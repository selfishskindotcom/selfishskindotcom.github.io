Get Your Beauty Sleep! Effects of Sleeping on Your Skin

Jan 4th, 2019 By Sylvia wrinkles

You must have grown up hearing to the phrase, You need to get your beauty sleep! commonly said by parents. The idiom beauty sleep is no longer a myth now since it's been proven that having a good night sleep is directly linked to health and skin. It's what you need to get through the day. Keep on reading as we unfold the beauty secrets to sleeping!

How is sleep related to skin?

Good quality sleep has a profound impact on the skin in many ways. When your body is in a state of recharge, a hormone is produced which repairs the damaged skin cells and restores the skin, making it look radiant and healthy. Not only has it provided skincare benefits, but it also keeps you healthy physically.

Sleep has many benefits - keeps your skin tight, reduces acne/blemishes/wrinkles and makes your skin appear youthful and radiant. Here is a breakdown of some of the benefits a good sleep has on skin:

Promotes glowing skin

The best night-time skincare routine starts with sleeping. A person suffering from sleep-deprivation would have dull skin and acne problems due to an imbalance of cortisol levels which leads to inflammation of the skin. Since lack of sleep is directly linked with the stress hormone, Cortisol can increase skin-sensitivity and dermatitis.

In addition to that, when your body is tired, the blood doesn’t circulate efficiently thus causing your skin to breakout. Having proper sleep leads to increased blood flow and while you’re breathing oxygen, your body releases out pollutants. This promotes restoration of the skin making it glow.

Regeneration of skin-cells

When your body shuts down to sleep, dead skin cells are shed off and replaced with new healthy skin cells. Your body starts to produce nutrients like collagen, elastin and hyaluronic acid - responsible for skin elasticity and firmness.

Keeping your skin elastic and firm prevents wrinkles, fine lines and sagging. The sleep hormone, melatonin is produced that acts as a barrier against premature aging.

Reduces dark circles around the eyes

While you are sleeping, the body restores hydration which means that your skin moisturizes and rejuvenates. Not getting enough sleep results in water imbalance, leading to swollen and puffy eyes as well as dryness.

Just like your body needs to stay hydrated, similarly, your skin needs water therapy to neutralize the negative effects.

Strengthens immune system

As alarming as it sounds, lack of sleep results in an increase in inflammatory cells that overcomes the functioning of the immune system. When your immune system gets weak, you’re prone to attract skin-related diseases such as Psoriasis and Eczema.

Getting quality sleep can help prevent inflammation and make you resilient against the diseases.

Better Sleep Better Skin

Here are some tips to sleep better:

  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep at night
  • Cut out alcoholic drinks and beverages before bedtime
  • Use a silk bed sheet as it has many skin benefits and helps you sleep comfortably
  • Keep electronic items and gadgets aside
  • Avoid heaving a heavy meal at night

Verdict

It’s safe to say that sleep contributes to a healthy and active lifestyle. Incorporating sleep into your skincare regimen doesn’t only help your skin, but it can boost your metabolism, weight, and health. Don’t underestimate the power of good beauty sleep!
