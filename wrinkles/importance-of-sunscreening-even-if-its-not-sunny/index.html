Importance of Sunscreening, Even If It's Not Sunny

Jan 18th, 2019 By Sylvia wrinkles

People use sunscreen throughout the summers and stop using it when winters arrive but is it a wise practice?

People use sunscreen throughout the summers and stop using it when winters arrive but is it a wise practice? Last time I checked, sunscreen is supposed to protect you from the harmful UV and UVB rays emitted by the Sun. Although you can’t see Sun on a cloudy day, it doesn’t mean that there are no rays. Experts say that you should wear sunscreen throughout the year and not just limit it to sunny days.

One thing is for certain that Sun is independent of hot and cold temperature conditions. You’re more likely to get tan or sunburn during winters the same way your skin is affected in summers.

Why sunscreen should be a part of skincare routine?

When you’re out in the daylight, your skin is exposed to harmful UV rays that cause skin aging problems while UVB rays cause burning of the skin. Sunscreen is important because it acts as a protective shield against the harmful rays detrimental to your skin. If you neglect the use of sunscreen, it would result in skin problems like skin pigmentation, wrinkles, tanning, and worst, cancer!

While tanned people are perceived as more attractive, tanning can damage the skin pigments called melanin responsible for protecting the skin from UV light thus potentially causing skin cancer.

It’s been proven that 25% of sun damage occurs before the age of 18 while the remaining percentage of damage occurs later as you get old. Therefore, it’s advised to wear sunscreen at all times.

The importance of sunscreen on cloudy days

According to research, 80% of the UV rays can penetrate through your skin on a cloudy day because clouds can only block the sun but not the UV rays. This is one of the common misconceptions of people. The rays can easily escape through a thick cloud and you can’t see it in the form of light because it’s radiation.

What about sunscreen in winters?

As a matter of fact, Snow and ice act as a mirror which can reflect up to 80% of UV rays resulting in skin damage. The sun rays are harmful even if you’re on the slopes doing skiing and snowboarding since UV radiation exposure increases 5% as you further go up the slope.

Sea and sand reflect up to 20% of UV rays which means that your skin is potentially at a higher risk in winters than in summers. So, it’s recommended to wear sunscreen in winters as well.

How to protect yourself from the Sun?

You need to apply sunblock on all areas that are exposed to the Sun. Purchase a broad-spectrum sunscreen that protects you from both UV and UVB rays. It should have an SPF of at least 30. If you’re out for a prolonged period of time, reapply it after every 1-2 hours.

Wear sunglasses to cover your eyes from the harmful radiation and wear long sleeved light colored clothes in summers to stay protected from the Sun.

Verdict

Adding sunscreen to your skincare routine is a smart choice. Whether you’re out at the beach, under the shade or even in your car make sure you’ve applied sunscreen so you could stay protected and cheerful.
